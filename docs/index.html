the problem

Syncing lyrics to audio is stubbornly manual.

Most people reach for a transcription model. On sung material it stumbles: held vowels stretch past what speech models expect, dense mixes mask consonants, ad-libs and vamps aren’t in any sheet. So you end up typing timestamps by hand, word by word.

the shift that fixes it

Don’t transcribe. Align.

Transcription asks what was sung — a hard question. Forced alignment is handed the lyrics and asks only when does each word occur — a far easier one. Run it on a separated vocal stem and the numbers change completely.

Bias-corrected, measured against human-tapped ground truth on the same audio and lyrics. Alignment takes about 8 seconds for 90 seconds of audio, on CPU.

how it works

Five stages, one command.

audio + lyrics.txt
  ↓  demucs separates the vocal
  ↓  CTC forced alignment places every word of your sheet
  ↓  a second pass re-times held notes the aligner rushes
  ↓  onsets snap to where the voice actually starts
  ↓  the run scores itself and flags what it doubts
timed.json · word SRT · line SRT · reference video

The SRTs import straight into Final Cut, Premiere, Resolve or After Effects. The styling stays yours — the built-in video is a plain reference render, not the product.

the part nobody ships

It tells you where it’s unsure.

  • Every word carries a confidence score, with thresholds computed per song — absolute cutoffs don’t transfer between a ballad and a rap.
  • Sung regions your sheet doesn’t cover are named out loud, quoting what was heard there — and the run aborts rather than silently showing a blank screen.
  • Ad-libs abstain instead of being forced into lyrics they don’t match.
  • Repeated lines already in your sheet are auto-recovered without bothering you.
$ lyric_lock run --audio song.wav --lyrics sheet.txt --separate

  CTC forced-align (MMS_FA) star=True display_lead=0.000s ...
  Path B sheet gate (preflight) ...
  GATE FAIL-LOUD 0.00-17.90s  MISSING_LYRICS
      heard: '...'
RuntimeError: sheet gate FAIL LOUD: sung region(s) missing from lyrics

When it can’t place a word honestly, it shows nothing rather than a lie.

hand-tuning, when you want it

The corrections are already queued.

Low-confidence words arrive pre-flagged in the browser bench. Press F to jump to the next thing the machine doubted; hold space through a word to re-time it — your tap snaps to the real onset, so rough timing is fine. Nudge, ripple a phrase, or edit the lyrics for a region, then export back to timed.json or SRT.

Most songs need none of this. It’s ready anyway.

The bench runs entirely in your browser — no server, nothing uploaded. Your masters never leave the machine.

honest limits

What it doesn’t do.

  • It needs the lyric text. It finds the timing, not the words. Lyrics-free transcription of singing is not a solved problem — our own falsified attempts are kept in the repo.
  • Very fast dense rap still jitters — word boundaries blur and function words collapse.
  • Backing vocals and parentheticals are skipped by design.
  • Validated by ear on a handful of songs, not on a public benchmark. Treat the numbers as measured-here, not as a leaderboard claim.
  • The default aligner weights are CC-BY-NC (non-commercial). A permissively licensed aligner can be swapped in — see NOTICE.md.

method

Negative results kept on purpose.

The repo keeps the failures: the falsified transcription path, the demoted repair mechanisms, and a display-lead constant that turned out to be captured human anticipation rather than a real preference. The reasoning that produced the working stack is in notes/eval/.
